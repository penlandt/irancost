# Data Sources

## War Cost Estimates
- **Pentagon briefing to Congress** (March 11, 2026) — Primary basis for the Phase 1 cost figure of $11.3B in the first six days. Reported via The Hill: https://thehill.com/homenews/5780153-operation-epic-fury-cost/
- **Center for Strategic and International Studies (CSIS)** — Published an estimate of $3.7B for the first 100 hours of Operation Epic Fury: https://www.csis.org/analysis/37-billion-estimated-cost-epic-furys-first-100-hours
- **Brown University Costs of War Project** — Provides a projected total range of $31–34B used to inform the longer-term rate model: https://costsofwar.watson.brown.edu/

## Supporting Contextual Data
- **AAA Gas Prices** — Daily national average gas prices: https://gasprices.aaa.com. Pre-war baseline: $2.98/gal (February 27, 2026).
- **BBC RSS Feed** — Live headlines pulled and cached every 5 minutes for the news ticker.
- **Casualty figures** — Manually updated from CENTCOM (https://www.centcom.mil), Iranian Red Crescent (https://www.en.ircs.ir), and IDF (https://www.idf.il/en/).
- **IRS filing season data** — 144.8M returns filed used for per-taxpayer calculation: https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/filing-season-statistics-by-year

## Source Philosophy
The site prioritizes publicly accessible, citable, and institutionally recognizable sources. No proprietary data feeds are used. All sources are linked directly on the site and in this repository.

## Update Notes
The war cost counter updates continuously using fixed model assumptions. Gas price and casualty data are updated manually as new figures become available. The BBC news ticker refreshes automatically every 5 minutes via server-side caching.
